How tagging works

The Explorer relies on community tagging to function. Every episode is connected to one or more Periods, Regions, Topics, and sometimes a Series. These tags power all filters, charts, groupings and statistics across the site.

To keep the dataset consistent, the tagging sheet uses locked columns and controlled dropdown lists. This ensures that values remain uniform — no duplicate spellings, variants or accidental new categories.

Period, Region & Topic

These fields use pre-defined multi-select dropdowns. You may select one or several values, but you cannot create new ones.

This prevents inconsistent tagging, such as:
• “Early Medieval” vs “Early-Medieval”
• “Middle East” vs “ME”
• “America” vs “The Americas”

Valid values are maintained centrally to keep filtering predictable and clean.

Series

The Series field is auto-suggested based on the episode title, but you may freely edit or correct it.

It is the only column that allows free text. All others are locked to protect the dataset from accidental edits.

Locked columns

Titles, descriptions, dates, episode IDs and all technical metadata are imported automatically from the public RSS feed and are locked for editing.

Only the tag fields described above are editable.

Access & contributions

The tagging sheet is read-only by default. To help maintain quality, editing is restricted to approved contributors.

Contribution philosophy

Tagging is collaborative. If you see something that looks incorrect, feel free to fix it — changes are tracked and several contributors help keep everything tidy.

The goal isn’t to create “perfect historical categorisation”, but a consistent structure that makes the Episode Explorer more useful to everyone.

Categories and tag data are based on a Google Sheet used by TRIH fans. This Explorer builds on the great work of Dan’s original categorisation (known on X as @Dan_YIMBY), now opening the tagging to the whole TRIH community.
